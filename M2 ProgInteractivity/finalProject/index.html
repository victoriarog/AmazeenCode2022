Dive into why orange cats have one orange braincell


by: Victoria Rogatinskaya, certified cat expert and conosseur



What is an orange cat?

Orange cat is a type of household cat, with distinctive orange/auburn coat colouring. Commonly acquired in dumpsters or any animal shelter if you are feeling fancy. Member of a Felis catus family of mammals, known to be our sidekicks since the dawn of times.



They are known for having exactly ONE braincell, that is orange and is commonly shared between all of them at any given moment, which makes for great, albeit concerning at times, companion for your daily struggles with reality. Members of Felis catus family are known to alleviate stress and bad moods by being cute, fluffy and absolutely adorable, but in turn are also infamous for wrecking havoc everywhere and screaming loudly when they want something from you. Life with them is but a mere balance.

So why one braincell?



"It has been proven with research, that all orange cats share one brain cell at any given moment, that just bounces around their head, something ado to a DVD screen saver." - Dr. Susanna Mewston, UCAT. - "Sometimes it hits a corner and a thought has a chance to form, although we are not certain as we always miss that moment."

As you read, scientists or, as we call them, catologists, aren't sure themselves of this peculiar phenomenon happening to our orange friends. We can, however, be certain that research is still ongoing and we are all eager to find out what is the root cause of this is. Another important question raised is, can an orange cat has no or negative number of braincells?
